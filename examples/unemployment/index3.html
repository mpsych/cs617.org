Labor force story prototype

Massachusetts unemployment rose higher than the nation during COVID—and sits above it again in early 2026

This prototype uses Chartist 1.5.0 and keeps the first chart as a broad overview, then uses the second chart to compare presidency periods inside this dataset.

A statewide shock, seen against the national curve

The first chart establishes the whole arc from 2020 to January 2026. Massachusetts is the visual focus; the U.S. remains the comparator.

The COVID spike becomes the key annotation moment

On scroll, the April 2020 peak appears as the first narrative callout. That is the cleanest “why this matters” entry point.

The Biden years enter as a contextual band, not as a separate chart

This keeps chart 1 honest. It still shows the full timeline, but it introduces the period that chart 2 will compare more directly.

The October 2025 reporting gap is visible and explained

The missing month stays missing. That builds trust and makes the government shutdown legible to the viewer.
